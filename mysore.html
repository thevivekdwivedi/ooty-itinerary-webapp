Bangalore → Mysore Road‑Trip Itinerary (18–20 Oct 2025)

Overview – route, weather and car suitability

The journey from Bangalore to Mysore via the modern NH‑275 expressway is only about 143 km and usually takes under three hours【736897614862978†L69-L83】. This highway is well maintained, making it comfortable for a sedan like your Skoda Superb. Mid‑October brings warm days (around 28 °C) with heavy showers – forecasts predict about 0.63 inch of rain on 18 Oct, 0.52 inch on 19 Oct and 0.48 inch on 20 Oct【71312127340625†L150-L157】, so pack umbrellas and plan indoor back‑ups.

This plan assumes you depart on the morning of 18 Oct 2025 and return by evening on 20 Oct 2025. Feel free to adjust times to match your pace.

Silent Shores Resort & Spa

Price & inclusions. Rates hover around US $140 per night (≈₹12,000) for a double room. This resort is not marketed as an all‑inclusive property; packages usually include breakfast only. Pets are generally not permitted. Availability: A review of major booking engines for the weekend of 18–20 Oct 2025 (two rooms for four adults) found only a few rooms left. Reserve early to guarantee your stay.

Why stay here? Set beside a picturesque lake on the outskirts of Mysore, Silent Shores offers modern rooms with private balconies overlooking the water【868779044617139†L157-L163】. Guests praise its peaceful setting【868779044617139†L157-L163】, and amenities include a swimming pool, spa, fitness centre, tennis courts and an international restaurant【868779044617139†L157-L163】. The quiet location makes it ideal for older travellers seeking relaxation, while younger couples appreciate the resort’s recreational facilities.

All‑inclusive options? We searched major travel sites and could not find a true all‑inclusive resort in Mysore. Silent Shores offers meal plans (bed & breakfast or half board), but not unlimited food and drinks. If an all‑inclusive experience is essential, consider the Hampi Heritage & Wilderness Resort, which has an all‑inclusive package including meals and safari【950518261969170†L166-L170】.

Suggested itinerary (with times)

  1. 18 Oct – Drive & heritage evening. 07:00 depart Bangalore. 09:30 coffee stop near Ramanagara. 10:00 check in at Silent Shores Resort and enjoy an early lunch. 15:00 head into town to tour the opulent Mysore Palace and stroll its art gallery. 18:00 return to the resort for dinner and a lakeside walk; alternatively, catch the sound‑and‑light show at the palace (if weather permits).
  2. 19 Oct – Hill, zoo and city tour. 06:30 start the day with a drive up Chamundi Hill – older guests can take a leisurely temple visit while younger visitors may climb the 1,000 steps. 08:30 enjoy breakfast back at the resort. 10:00 explore Mysore Zoo (Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens) – one of India’s oldest zoos. 12:30 return to the resort for lunch or sample a local café. 14:30 visit St. Philomena’s Cathedral, then wander through Devaraja Market for spices, flowers and crafts. 18:00 unwind at the resort’s spa; dine at the on‑site restaurant.
  3. 20 Oct – Gardens & return. 08:00 breakfast and checkout. 09:00 drive to Brindavan Gardens (KRS Dam) to enjoy the symmetric lawns and musical fountain. 11:00 begin the drive back to Bangalore with a lunch stop near Maddur. Reach home by 15:00–16:00.

Note: This itinerary balances gentle sightseeing with more active options to suit both older and younger travellers. Always check live weather updates; heavy rain may warrant swapping outdoor sites for indoor attractions like the Rail Museum, Jaganmohan Palace Art Gallery or shopping centres.
